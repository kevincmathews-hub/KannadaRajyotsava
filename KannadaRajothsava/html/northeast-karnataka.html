About North-East Karnataka

Kalyana Karnataka, formerly known as Hyderabad Karnataka, is a culturally rich and historically significant region located in the northeastern part of Karnataka. The region comprises seven districts ‚Äî Bidar, Kalaburagi (Gulbarga), Yadgir, Raichur, Koppal, Ballari, and Vijayanagara. It was once under the rule of the Nizams of Hyderabad before becoming part of Karnataka after the reorganization of states in 1956. The name "Kalyana Karnataka" is derived from the historic city of Basavakalyan, which was a prominent center of the 12th-century Bhakti movement led by the social reformer Basaveshwara, who advocated equality, education, and social justice.

The region holds immense historical and cultural importance. It was a cradle of the Vachana movement, which shaped Kannada literature and philosophy. Monuments like Bidar Fort, Gol Gumbaz in Bijapur (nearby region), and Basavakalyan Fort reflect the architectural grandeur of medieval Deccan kingdoms. The area also showcases a unique blend of Kannada and Deccani cultures, with influences from the Chalukyas, Bahmanis, and the Nizams ‚Äî making it a mosaic of languages, traditions, and cuisines.

Economically and educationally, Kalyana Karnataka has been a focus area for development, as it historically lagged behind other regions of the state due to its feudal past under Nizam rule. In 2019, the Kalyana Karnataka Region Development Board (KKRDB) was established under Article 371(J) of the Indian Constitution, which provides special provisions for education, employment, and infrastructure. Today, the region is steadily progressing, with its rich heritage and emerging opportunities making it a vital part of Karnataka's cultural and developmental landscape.
