Where Rani Chennamma's courage and the Tricolor's spirit unite!

Origin and Significance

Kittur Karnataka derives its name from the historic town Kittur in Belagavi district, home to Rani Chennamma, one of India's first freedom fighters (1824). Earlier called Hyderabad-Karnataka, the region was renamed in 2021 to honor her bravery. It includes Belagavi, Bagalkote, Dharwad, Gadag, Haveri, Vijayapura, and Uttara Kannada districts ‚Äî each rich in art, architecture, and tradition.

Culture and Traditions

Folk Dances: Dollu Kunitha, Veeragase, Goravara Kunitha, and Lavani.

Art and Handicrafts: Ilkal Sarees (Bagalkot), Bidriware (Bidar), and Lambani Embroidery.

Cuisine: Jolada Rotti, Ennegayi (stuffed brinjal), Shenga Chutney, Karadantu (Gokak), and Belagavi Kunda.

Festivals: Kittur Utsav, Dasara, Deepavali, and Basava Jayanti celebrated with grandeur.

Hubballi ‚Äì Where the Tricolor is Born

Hubballi in Dharwad district is India's only National Flag manufacturing center. The Karnataka Khadi Gramodyoga Samyukta Sangha (KKGSS) in Hubballi is the only BIS-authorized organization to produce the Indian National Flag. Every Tricolor that flies proudly across the nation ‚Äî from government offices to the Red Fort ‚Äî is woven here with devotion.

"From the Land of Rani Chennamma, the Tricolor takes flight!"

Famous Places to Visit

Kittur Fort and Palace (Belagavi) ‚Äì Symbol of Rani Chennamma's valor.

Badami, Aihole, Pattadakal (Bagalkot) ‚Äì The cradle of Indian temple architecture.

Gokak Falls (Belagavi) ‚Äì The Niagara of Karnataka.

Dandeli (Uttara Kannada) ‚Äì Wildlife and adventure destination.

Vijayapura (Bijapur) ‚Äì Gol Gumbaz, the second-largest dome in the world, and Ibrahim Roza, said to be an inspiration for the Taj Mahal in Agra.

Districts of Kittur Karnataka and Their Taglines

Belagavi - Sugar Bowl of Karnataka - Known for Belagavi Kunda and Gokak Falls.

Dharwad - Cultural Capital of Karnataka - Famous for Hindustani music and education.

Hubballi - Commercial Hub of North Karnataka - Home of India's National Flag factory (KKGSS).

Gadag - Mudrana Nagari (Printing City) - Famous for printing presses and Lakkundi temples.

Haveri - Land of Cardamom and Canaries - Birthplace of poet Kanakadasa.

Bagalkot - Cradle of Chalukyan Architecture - Home to Aihole, Badami, and Pattadakal temples.

Vijayapura (Bijapur) - City of Monuments - Renowned for Gol Gumbaz and Islamic architecture.

Essence of Kittur Karnataka
